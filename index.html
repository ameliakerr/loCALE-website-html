The LoCALE Project

About the Project

The Local Crime and Law Enforcement Project (LoCALE) is a national database compiling micro-level arrest, incident, and dispatch records from thousands of cities across the United States. Designed for researchers and policymakers, LoCALE provides a more comprehensive and granular view of public safety activity than existing crime data sources.

Current crime datasets face significant limitations: coverage is often restricted to select jurisdictions, data rarely extends below the city level, low-level offenses such as theft are frequently excluded, offender characteristics are limited, and there is no centralized source for dispatch data. These gaps constrain rigorous research and evidence-based policymaking.

LoCALE addresses these shortcomings by expanding geographic coverage to the census tract level, incorporating both high- and low-level offenses, and including richer information on offender characteristics. It is also the first centralized source for dispatch data across jurisdictions, enabling new lines of inquiry into law enforcement response.

By integrating arrest, incident, and dispatch records into a unified framework, LoCALE aims to support more precise measurement, deeper analysis, and more informed public policy.
